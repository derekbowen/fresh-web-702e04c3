The #1 reason hosts get shut down isn't safety or insurance. It's a neighbor complaint. The fix is simple and almost no one does it.

Before your next booking:

  • Knock on the doors of your two closest neighbors. Tell them you're renting the pool out occasionally on weekends. Ask them to text you directly if anything is ever too loud.
  • Give them your cell. That single gesture defuses 90% of future complaints.
  • Bring a small gift the first time. A bottle of wine, a gift card. $20 spent here saves $2,000 of headaches.

House rules that prevent complaints:

  • No music from outdoor speakers after 8pm
  • No glass containers in the pool area
  • Max guest count strictly enforced (you set this)
  • Parking only in the driveway, never blocking neighbors

If you have an HOA, check the bylaws on short-term use. Most allow daytime guest use without issue. A short, friendly note to the HOA board before you start ("just want to keep you in the loop") goes a long way.
